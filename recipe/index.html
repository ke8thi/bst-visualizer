Ingredients & Equipment

Ingredients

  1. Instant Coffee: The star of this recipe—using good-quality instant coffee is key.
  2. Sugar: Provides the sweetness and helps create the perfect foam.
  3. Hot Water: Essential to dissolve the coffee and sugar for that instant coffee foam.
  4. Milk: Choose your favorite dairy or non-dairy milk for a customized flavor.
  5. Optional: Flavored syrups, cocoa powder, or a pinch of salt to enhance the taste.

Equipment

  1. Mixing Bowl: A deep bowl works best.
  2. Whisk or Electric Mixer: To whip the mixture until it reaches a creamy consistency
    3. .
  3. Spoon: For layering the whipped coffee over milk.
  4. Serving Glass: Choose a clear glass to showcase the layered effect
This is a homemade Dalgona coffee recipe with minimal ingredients, ensuring that you can make Dalgona coffee at home with instant coffee without any fuss.

Step-by-Step Instruction

1. Prepare Your Ingredients
Measure equal parts of instant coffee, sugar, and hot water. For example, start with 2 tablespoons of each. This simple whipped coffee recipe ensures a balanced flavor and creamy texture.
2. Whip the Mixture
Using a whisk or electric mixer, vigorously whip the instant coffee, sugar, and hot water. Keep going until you achieve a thick, frothy, and velvety mixture. This is the instant coffee foam that makes your Dalgona coffee recipe truly special. Remember, this is a step-by-step Dalgona coffee recipe for beginners, so patience is key if the mixture seems too runny, keep whipping!
3. Assemble the Drink
Fill a glass with cold or hot milk according to your preference. Then, gently spoon the creamy whipped coffee topping over the milk. You’ll notice the beautiful layered effect that’s become synonymous with the viral coffee recipe trend.
Enjoy your barista-style coffee at home with this authentic Dalgona coffee recipe for a café experience.

Tips for Perfecting Your Dalgona Coffee

  1. Achieve the Right Consistency: If your mixture isn’t thick enough, continue whipping until you see stiff peaks.
  2. Temperature Tips: Use freshly boiled water and chilled milk for a striking contrast.
  3. Ingredient Variations: Experiment with non-dairy milk, sugar alternatives, or add a dash of cocoa for an extra twist.
  4. Troubleshooting: If your whipped coffee topping is too stiff, a small splash of water can help achieve the desired creamy texture.
